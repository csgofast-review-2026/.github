## Game Modes Explained in Detail

The platform offers several established CS2 skin gambling formats. Below is a closer look at how each mode works and what kind of gameplay experience it provides.

### Crash

Crash is a multiplier-based game built around timing. A multiplier starts at a low value and increases continuously until it crashes at a randomly generated point.

Players place a bet before the round begins and must cash out before the crash occurs. If the multiplier crashes before cashout, the bet is lost. The longer a player waits, the higher the potential return, but also the higher the risk.

Crash is designed for fast-paced sessions and appeals to users who prefer quick resolution and visible volatility.

### Case Battles

Case Battles allow multiple players to open identical cases simultaneously. The total value of the items received determines the winner.

Instead of playing purely against probability, participants compete against each other. This adds a competitive layer and shifts focus from multiplier timing to item valuation.

Results are displayed immediately after case openings, and value comparison is transparent.

### Double

Double is a simplified roulette-style mode. A spinning wheel lands on one of two dominant colors, with a smaller segment representing a less frequent outcome.

Players bet on a chosen color before the spin begins. The structure is straightforward, rounds resolve quickly, and odds remain fixed for each segment.

Double is typically used for short, repetitive sessions due to its simplicity.

### Roulette

Roulette expands on the Double format with additional segments and outcome combinations. Players can distribute bets across different options depending on probability distribution.

The outcome is determined by a randomized spin, and the result is displayed immediately. The mechanics are predictable and easy to understand.

Roulette provides slightly more variety while maintaining a clear probability structure.

### Hi-Lo

Hi-Lo is a progression-based card game. A starting card is shown, and players predict whether the next card will be higher or lower.

Each correct prediction increases potential payout. Players can stop at any stage to secure winnings or continue to increase exposure.

The game emphasizes risk management rather than speed.

### Tower

Tower is a step-by-step elimination game. Players select safe positions in each row, progressing upward while avoiding hidden losing tiles.

Each successful step increases potential reward. The player may stop at any time to secure the current payout.

Tower is structured around incremental decision-making and controlled exposure.

### Wheel

Wheel is a circular probability game where different segments represent different multipliers.

Players place bets on selected segments before the spin. The wheel lands on a multiplier, determining the payout.

The mode combines visible probability distribution with simple betting mechanics, making it easy to understand while still offering variable risk.

Each mode follows transparent rules. Results resolve immediately and are recorded in accessible histories.

Fast-paced modes such as Crash emphasize volatility, while step-based formats like Hi-Lo and Tower provide incremental decision-making. This balance supports different risk preferences without altering core mechanics.
